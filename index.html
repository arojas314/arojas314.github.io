About Me

Alex Rojas, M.S.

Geospatial Data Analyst

I am a geospatial data analyst specializing in remote sensing applications. My research interests include Geographic Information Science (GIS), remote sensing, and environmental modeling. More specifically, I am interested in applying geospatial big data and machine learning to study environmental change, especially forest and wetland dynamics.
My recent work involves using airborne and satellite remote sensing data to derive various forest structural parameters and quantities, such as above ground biomass, foliage clumping, canopy albedo, and vertical foliage profiles. Specifically, this work contributes to the ability to predict terrestrial carbon storage and fluxes in response to anthropogenic and natural environmental change.

Mapping Aboveground Biomass in the Continental U.S. using GEDI Waveforms

In this research, we provide estimates of above ground biomass across the continental U.S. using a full waveform based model. The model utilizes the full vertical information derived from waveforms, integrating from the bottom to the top of the canopy layer the product of the waveform and a power function of height. The model is parameterized using in-situ vegetation structure data provided by the NSF NEON program and compared with FIA AGB estimates. Theoretical basis paper.

Modeling Gross Primary Production from OCO-3 Solar Induced Flourescence (SIF) and CO2 Measurements

Utilized OCO-3 measurements to estimate diurnal and seasonal variability of CO2 concentration by land cover type and estimate GPP at three major metropolitan areas.

Quantifying Above-Ground Biomass in Tropical Forests Directly from Lidar Waveforms

Estimating above ground biomass using full waveform lidar measurements in tropical forests in Gabon, Central Africa. Theoretical basis paper.

U-NET CNN Semantic Segmentation of Land Cover Types using Airborne Imagery in Dubai

Utilizing airborne imagery from Dubai hosted on Kaggle, a large depth U-NET CNN model was trained for semantic segmentation on a simple 5 class land cover dataset.
